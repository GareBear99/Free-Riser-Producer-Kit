## 🎛️ DAW Setup

### FL Studio
1. Drag WAV into a **Sampler** or directly onto the **playlist**
2. For BPM-synced files — set project BPM to match the file name before dropping in
3. Use the **Mixer** to route into a reverb send for tail extension

### Ableton Live
1. Drag WAV into an **Audio Track**
2. For BPM-synced files — right-click → **Warp** → set to your session BPM
3. Use **Clip Automation** to automate volume/filter for extra control

### Logic Pro X
1. Drag WAV into the **timeline**
2. Use **Flex Time** on BPM-synced files if your session BPM differs
3. Route through **Space Designer** for room/reverb tail

### MPC / Maschine
1. Load as a one-shot pad sample
2. Trigger on beat 1 of the 4-bar lead-in
3. Let it breathe — don't gate it

## 💡 Production Tips

- **Layer two risers** — pair a noise riser with a synth riser at -6dB. The noise adds air, the synth adds pitch movement. Together they're enormous.
- **Reverse a Whoosh** — take `Riser_Whoosh_4s.wav`, reverse it in your DAW, and use it as a downlifter after the drop.
- **Pitch the Supersaw** — pitch `Riser_Supersaw` up or down in semitones to match your track key. It'll lock in and feel intentional.
- **Sidechain the riser to your kick** — let the kick duck the riser on beat 4 for a brief "breathe" before the drop hits.
- **Stack Snare Roll + White Noise** — the snare gives rhythm, the noise gives frequency mass. Classic combo for any genre.
- **Sub Rumble in mono only** — `Riser_Sub_Rumble` should be hard mono. Sub frequencies in stereo cause phase issues on club systems.
- **Use the 2s versions for fills** — `_2s` risers are perfect for 2-bar drops, FX hits, and in-phrase tension moments, not just full 4-bar builds.
- **Bitcrush into a drop** — `Riser_Bitcrush_4s` works great feeding into a lo-fi or phonk drop — the resolution climb builds anticipation in a unique way.

## 🎚️ Mixing Notes

| Riser Type | EQ Suggestion | Reverb | Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Noise risers | High-shelf boost at 8kHz | Short plate | −6 to −3 dBFS |
| Synth sweeps | Cut mud below 80Hz | Medium hall | −9 to −6 dBFS |
| Drum risers | Leave flat | Minimal — keep it dry | −6 to −3 dBFS |
| FX/Texture | Trust your ears | Match room to track | −12 to −6 dBFS |
| Cinematic | Cut below 40Hz | Long hall | −9 to −6 dBFS |
| Sub Rumble | Cut above 150Hz | None — keep dry | −6 dBFS mono |
